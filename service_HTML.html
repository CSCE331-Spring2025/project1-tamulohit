Robo Mania STEM Learning Center Counselor

Over the summer after my freshman year, I tutored at a local robotics camp to get back in touch with my engineering roots and help a new generation of designers and thinkers. Robo Mania STEM Learning was deeply nostalgic and akin to the various workshops I participated in for robotics and coding at a young age. Teaching kids essential concepts and promoting the method of thinking posed a wonderful experience and challenge! The joy and the “Aha!” moment on their faces when they solved that immovable problem brought deep content and reason for me to be patient and guiding with the students. This experience helped the students become better thinkers and exposed them to a world of possibilities, the very same world that led me here!

Leander ISD Superintendent Student Council Member

This opportunity came to me unexpectedly, as it is one where you are selected based on the nominations of others. Thus, I had the unique and humble chance to present ideas directly to the superintendent of Leander ISD along with some other students in the district. Realizing the gravitas of this position, I spent much time deliberating over what problems to shine light on. In the end, I realized that the integration of soft skill development was severely lacking in most classroom curricula, and made efforts to promote its importance to the council. We made a year long onboarding plan to incorporate lessons on soft skills and building comfort for in class presentations. We made it certain that the integration is gradual and relevant to the class curriculums, as the intention is for students to understand the omnipresence of communication without getting overwhelmed or anxious.
